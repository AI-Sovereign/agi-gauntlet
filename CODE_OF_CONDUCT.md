# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

In the interest of fostering an open and welcoming environment, we as contributors and maintainers pledge to making participation in our project and our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We are building the architecture for Artificial General Intelligence. Our focus must remain on logic, mathematics, and code. Ego has no compile time.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to creating a positive environment include:
* Using welcoming and inclusive language.
* Being respectful of differing viewpoints, even when debating the definitions of AGI, sentience, or neuro-symbolic theory.
* Gracefully accepting constructive criticism of your code or mathematical proofs.
* Focusing on what is best for the community and the integrity of the benchmark.

Examples of unacceptable behavior by participants include:
* Trolling, insulting/derogatory comments, and public or private harassment.
* Deliberately attempting to sabotage the benchmark by publicizing obfuscated prompts.
* Arrogance that overshadows technical merit.
* Any other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional scientific environment.

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported by contacting the project team. The AGI Systems Directorate maintainers retain the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned to this Code of Conduct, or to ban temporarily or permanently any contributor for other behaviors that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

Measure the code, not the person.
